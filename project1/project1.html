Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

1875-1950

Iron Man Of India

Sardar Vallabhbhai Jhaverbhai Patel was an Indian independence nationalist and barrister who served as the first Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister of India from 1947 to 1950. He was a senior leader of the Indian National Congress, who played a significant role in the country's struggle for independence and its political integration. In India and elsewhere, he was often called Sardar, meaning "Chief" in Hindi, Urdu, Bengali and Persian. He acted as the Home Minister during the political integration of India and the Indo-Pakistani War of 1947.

Biograpies :

  • Early Life: Vallabhbhai Patel was born on October 31, 1875, in Nadiad, Gujarat, British India. He belonged to a farmer family and had a modest upbringing. He completed his primary education in Karamsad and later pursued law at the Inns of Court in London.
  • Legal Career: After returning from England, Patel started his legal practice in Ahmedabad, Gujarat. He gained a reputation for his brilliance and integrity as a lawyer and became a successful barrister. However, he soon realized the call of the nation and decided to dedicate himself to public service.
  • Political Journey: Patel entered politics during the Indian National Congress (INC) sessions in the early 1910s. He rose to prominence through his effective leadership and organizational skills. Patel played a crucial role in the Bardoli Satyagraha of 1928, a successful nonviolent resistance movement against unfair taxation by the British.
  • Role in Indian Independence: Sardar Patel became an integral part of the Indian National Congress and actively participated in the fight for India's independence from British colonial rule. He played a pivotal role in the negotiations with the British government and was a key figure in the formulation of the Mountbatten Plan, which led to the partition of India.
  • Formation of the Indian Administrative Services: Patel recognized the need for a strong and efficient administrative system in the newly independent India. He played a key role in establishing the Indian Administrative Services (IAS) and the Indian Police Services (IPS) to provide a competent and dedicated civil service.
  • Statue of Unity: In his honor, the Statue of Unity, the world's tallest statue, was erected near the Narmada River in the state of Gujarat. The statue stands as a symbol of Patel's monumental contribution to the nation and serves as a tourist attraction and reminder of his legacy.
